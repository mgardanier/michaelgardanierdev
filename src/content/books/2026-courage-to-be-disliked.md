A dive into Adlerian psychology, structured as a conversation between a teacher and a student. The adversarial tone between the two helped drive the narrative, however sometimes it did feel a little contrived and unrealistic in terms of how the two interacted with each other.

#### Favorite Thoughts
* Talked about the main guiding star “The key to happiness is contributing to others”, or the idea that you feel joy when you feel you are contributing to the life of someone else. It doesn’t even have to be true, it can be totally subjective.
*	Separation of Tasks” – the idea of only focusing on the things you can control, in all relationships. Even when it comes to parent/child relationships, a parent shouldn’t try to influence the child's tasks (doing homework, chores, etc). Instead you should trust them to take responsibility for their own tasks.
	*	Also talks about having peer relationships instead of hierarchical relationships. In the child example, it said showing gratitude is important and condescension is harmful. I’m not sure how much I follow or understand this.
	*	This idea of equal relationships avoids instilling the “desire/pursuit of recognition” in others.
*	Freedom is being disliked. When you don’t care what others think of you, you are free. If someone dislikes you because you chose to live according to your own principles or goals, then you are free. If you always try to make others happy, you are not free. Seeking praise or acceptance is the opposite of freedom.
	* It comes back to the separation of tasks – what someone thinks of you is their task not yours.
